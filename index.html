Understanding Corruption

What is Corruption?

Corruption is a complex and pervasive issue that undermines the principles of fairness, transparency, and accountability in society. It involves the abuse of entrusted power for private gain, whether it occurs in government institutions, businesses, or other organizations. Corruption can take various forms, such as bribery, embezzlement, nepotism, and abuse of authority.

Bribery, for example, is one of the most common forms of corruption. It occurs when an individual or entity offers, gives, receives, or solicits something of value in order to influence the action of an official in the discharge of their public or legal duties. Embezzlement involves the misappropriation of funds that have been entrusted to a person, often within a business or organizational setting.

Corruption has far - reaching consequences. It erodes public trust in institutions, hinders economic development, and perpetuates social inequality. It diverts resources away from essential services such as healthcare, education, and infrastructure, and can lead to a breakdown of the rule of law.
